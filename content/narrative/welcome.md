Welcome to Governing Ground! This is an in-progress digital history project focused on conflicts over agriculture and land use between 1937 and 1997, with a particular emphasis on the [Sagebrush Rebellion](/narrative/introduction/).

Some key features of the project: 

- **[Sightlines](/sightlines/)** are data visualizations built out of open data sources and historical research. The section is divided into themes or events as well as geographic filters, allowing readers to see only those visualizations that are relevant to an area of interest. Each Sightline also has its own self-contained narrative section describing what I think are the important takeaways.
- For any data I've compiled and that I can share publicly I've put together on an **[open data](/data/)** page describing how those datasets came together and what they contain. Every piece of open data comes with its own Python scripts for replicability.
- This section here---the **[writing](/narrative/)** section---will serve a few different goals. The first is simply narrative history, to give context to the historical events, data, and people that comprise the histories I'm seeking to understand. Second, this space will serve as a kind of research diary space where I'll share sources I find interesting or report on research trips.
- The **[bibliography](/sources/)** is compiled from my Zotero library and I'll occasionally update this as my Zotero bibliography grows.
- In a matter of transparency, I've included a **[generative AI statement](/ai/)** to indicate what parts of this project were created with the aid of an agent and---more importantly---which sections were not. In short, the starter design and scripts for working with open data were created by Claude Sonnet but any piece of writing---on this page, in data visualization narratives, or any other page that contains text---was all written by me.
- The site includes a **[changelog](/changelog])** to document when things about the site change. This is intentionally capacious: design updates, new visualizations, additions to narratives, and even minor changes will be documented here. Occasionally these may rise to the level of something akin to a methodology white paper, in which case they'll get their own page on the site.
